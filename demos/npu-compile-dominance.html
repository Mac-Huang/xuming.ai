Does FeasibleAllocation actually dominate NPU compile time?

A controlled sweep on Intel NPU 5 (OpenVINO 2026.0) across Qwen2 (dense attn), ViT (vision encoder) · 20 cold-compile configurations · per-pass timing from the VPUX compiler's own report.

Short answer: no — not in general.

FeasibleAllocation (the memory allocator + list-scheduler) is the single heaviest pass in only 4 of 20 configurations. In 11 of 20 the heaviest pass is MultiClusterStrategyAssignment — the tiling / multi-cluster strategy search. Across the whole sweep FA ranges just 4.46%–33.36% of pass time. It only takes over at large model × large window: it needs ~7B params and a long prompt window before it dominates (reaching ~33% of the whole compile, ~44% at the stage level).

FA is #1 only ≥7B & W≥512 tiling-search #1 for every small/mid compile ELF-serialize ≈ 20–28% floor everywhere FA share flat ~5–8% for ≤3B at any window

The crossover: which stage owns the compile

Ordering every configuration by cold compile time (cheap → expensive) shows a clean hand-off. Tiling + strategy search owns the cheap compiles (~24–34%) and shrinks with scale; memory allocation + scheduling starts at ~11% and climbs to ~44% on the most expensive ones. ELF serialization is a steady floor throughout. FeasibleAllocation isn't a fixed bottleneck — it's the pass whose cost scales.

Why it scales: size × window, not modality

Within the dense Qwen2 family the FA share is flat ~5–8% up to 3B regardless of window, then on the 7B it climbs 5% → 10% → 33% as the window goes 512 → 1024. So two things must both be large — parameters and prompt window — because FeasibleAllocation schedules every tiled sub-operation through the NPU's tiny CMX, and the op count grows with both. ViT (a vision encoder, no KV cache) sits with the small models; it's size, not the attention-vs-vision distinction, that moves the needle.

What this says to optimize

The bottleneck is regime-specific, so the lever depends on your workload:

  • Small/mid models (≤3B, any window): the target is the tiling / multi-cluster strategy search — and it runs fully serial ( MultiClusterStrategyAssignment user/wall = 1.0×), so it won't get faster on more cores. Caching or pruning the strategy search is the lever.
  • Large models with long windows (≥7B, W≥1024): the target is FeasibleAllocation — a greedy simulate-and-spill list scheduler that re-runs 2–3× and is only mildly parallel. Bounding the window or shipping precompiled blobs keyed by (model, W) avoids it entirely.
  • Every compile: ELF serialization is an unavoidable ~20–28% floor — the cheapest win is not recompiling at all (warm blob cache).
