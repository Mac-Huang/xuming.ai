I am a Junior Comp Sci Student at University of Wisconsin - Madison, where I work on Machine Learning Systems, Operating Systems, and Efficient Computing Infrastructure.

I'm an innovative CS undergrad exploring the cutting edge where AI meets systems, passionate about transforming bold ideas into rigorous research. My work sits at the intersection of artificial intelligence, systems, and security, focusing on leveraging machine learning to enhance the reliability and security of critical system software.

Currently advised by Prof. Remzi Arpaci-Dusseau and Vinay Banakar at The ADvanced Systems Laboratory, I'm developing LinuxGuard—exploring how large language models and RAG-enhanced architectures can automate vulnerability detection in the Linux kernel, transforming months of manual security analysis into hours of automated discovery.

During my Stanford summer session, I achieved A+ (99/100) in CS107 Computer Organization & Systems and A (93/100) in CS161 Algorithms. While there, I discovered critical access control misconfigurations in Stanford's AFS directories—world-readable solution binaries and exposed teaching materials across multiple courses (CS107, MSANDE131, POLISCI214, SURG211A). After verifying these vulnerabilities ethically, I documented them in a technical report and submitted responsible disclosures to course instructors and Stanford's Information Security Office. This experience crystallized my commitment to systems security research, directly inspiring my current work on automated vulnerability detection.
